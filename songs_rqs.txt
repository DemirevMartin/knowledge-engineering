# REVISED RESEARCH QUESTIONS
## Knowledge Engineering Project: Grammy Nominees & Production Characteristics

---

## CONTEXT & SCOPE

**Data:** Grammy Awards 1958–2024 + WASABI songs (2.1M) + Last.fm engagement
**Coverage:** ~30–40% of Grammy work nominations (primarily Pop, Rock, R&B, Country, Hip-Hop, Electronic)
**Analysis focus:** Genre associations, production characteristics (BPM, loudness), listener engagement
**Limitation:** Classical, Jazz, Gospel, World Music underrepresented in Last.fm; perceptual audio features unavailable

---

## REVISED RQ1 (Genre-Category Association)

### **RQ1: How do genre associations with Grammy categories evolve across decades, and which (genre, category) associations are most prevalent among Grammy-nominated works?**

**What this asks:**
- Not "which genres are common" (too simple) → now asking about *temporal patterns* and *association strength*
- What is the genre distribution for Pop nominees in the 1980s vs. 2020s?
- Which (genre, category) pairs are frequent, rare, or non-existent?
- Are certain genres *exclusive* to specific categories (e.g., is "Country" only associated with Country category)?

**Knowledge Graph aspect:**
- Nodes: Grammy categories, genres, decades
- Edges: "is_nominated_in", "associated_with", "prevalent_in"
- Queries: "Which genres are *most associated* with each Grammy category?" "How has the genre composition of Rock nominees changed 1960–2024?"

**Analytical method:**
- Frequency tables: (category, genre) cross-tabulation
- Decade-wise comparison: chi-square tests for independence
- Association strength: Jaccard similarity, cosine similarity between category-genre distributions
- Visualization: heatmaps of (category, genre) prevalence by decade

**Why this is knowledge engineering:**
You're building a *knowledge model* of "what genres *associate with* what categories", not predicting which genre will be nominated next.

## REVISED RQ2 (Engagement & Production by Outcome)

### **RQ2: Within each Grammy category, how do the genre profiles, listener engagement metrics (Last.fm listeners/playcount), and production characteristics (BPM, loudness) of nominated works *associate with* Grammy outcome (winner vs. non-winning nominee)?**

**What this asks:**
- Not "do winners have higher popularity?" (too outcome-focused) → now asking about *characteristic associations*
- For Rock nominees: what is the typical genre mix, engagement level, and production profile for winners vs. nominees?
- Are these associations *category-specific* or *universal across categories*?
- Example: "Do Rock winners characteristically have higher loudness than Rock nominees?" vs. "Do winners universally have higher loudness?"

**Knowledge Graph aspect:**
- Nodes: Grammy categories, winner/nominee status, genre, engagement levels (low/medium/high), BPM ranges, loudness ranges
- Edges: "has_outcome", "characterizes", "associated_with"
- Queries: "What is the characteristic genre diversity of Country winners?" "Which production profiles associate with winning outcomes in each category?"

**Analytical method:**
- Stratified analysis by category (separate findings for Pop, Rock, R&B, etc.)
- Distribution comparison: genre diversity (Shannon entropy) between winners/nominees per category
- Engagement analysis: median listeners/playcount for winners vs. nominees per category
- Production characteristic analysis: BPM/loudness distributions (box plots, medians, IQR) by outcome per category
- Association testing: Kruskal-Wallis tests (non-parametric, appropriate for limited features)
- Document findings as "*characteristics that associate with* winning" not "*predictors of* winning"

**Why this is knowledge engineering:**
You're documenting what *production and engagement profiles characteristically look like* for different outcomes in different categories, not building a classifier.

**Important caveat on Last.fm skew:**
- Explicitly stratify by category and note: "High-coverage categories (Pop, Rock, R&B, Country, Hip-Hop) have robust engagement data; Jazz, Classical, Gospel have limited Last.fm representation and are excluded from engagement analysis."
- This is methodologically honest, not a failure.

## REVISED RQ3 (Multi-Category Artists & Sonic Consistency)

### **RQ3: Which Grammy-nominated artists span multiple categories, and how do their genre selections, listener engagement, and production characteristics (BPM, loudness) *vary or remain consistent* across the categories they span?**

**What this asks:**
- Not "how do features differ" (descriptive) → now asking about *consistency patterns* and *category adaptation*
- Example: Does Taylor Swift's nominated works in Pop have different BPM/loudness than her nominated works in Country?
- Do multi-category artists "adapt their sound" per category, or maintain a consistent sonic signature?
- What does the *genre diversity* look like within a single artist's multi-category nominations?

**Knowledge Graph aspect:**
- Nodes: Artists, Grammy categories, genres, BPM ranges, loudness ranges, engagement levels
- Edges: "nominated_in", "characterized_by", "varies_across", "maintains_signature"
- Queries: "For artists nominated in both Rock and Pop, how do their genre selections differ?" "What is the BPM consistency within an artist's category-spanning nominations?"

**Analytical method:**
- Identify artists with nominations in ≥2 categories
- Within-artist comparison: for each artist, compute variance/consistency of:
  - Genre distribution across their categories
  - Median BPM in each of their categories
  - Median loudness in each of their categories
  - Median listeners/playcount in each of their categories
- Clustering: group artists by their "consistency pattern" (e.g., "adaptive" vs. "signature-consistent")
- Control for confounds: is variance due to release year effects? Category norms?

**Why this is knowledge engineering:**
You're building a *knowledge model* of "which artists maintain sonic consistency" vs. "which artists adapt per category", and documenting those patterns as a knowledge structure.

## SUMMARY: How These RQs Work Together

| RQ | Scope | Knowledge Model | Output |
|-----|-------|-----------------|--------|
| **RQ1** | All Grammy-nominated works in dataset | "What genres *associate with* what categories across time?" | Association network: category ↔ genre, temporal evolution |
| **RQ2** | All Grammy-nominated works, stratified by category | "What production/engagement profiles *characterize* winners vs. nominees?" | Category-specific profiles: "Rock winners are characterized by X genre mix, Y engagement, Z production range" |
| **RQ3** | Multi-category artists only | "Which artists maintain sonic signatures vs. adapt per category?" | Artist clusters: "Adaptive artists" (e.g., Swift) vs. "Signature artists" (e.g., Radiohead) |

## Key Language Shifts for Knowledge Engineering

### ❌ Avoid (Predictive/Causal framing):
- "predict which artists will win"
- "determine whether loudness causes winning"
- "what factors drive Grammy success"

### ✅ Use (Associative/Descriptive framing):
- "what production characteristics *associate with* Grammy winners in each category"
- "how do genres *characteristically distribute* across categories"
- "in what ways do multi-category artists *vary or remain consistent*"
- "what is the knowledge graph structure of (artist, category, genre, production)?"
- "document the characteristic profiles of winners vs. nominees"

## Knowledge Graph Schema (Sketch)

```
ENTITIES:
  - Grammy_Category (Pop, Rock, R&B, Country, Hip-Hop, Electronic, ...)

  - Artist
  - Work (song/album)
  - Decade (1960s, 1970s, ..., 2020s)

  - nominated_in(Work, Grammy_Category, Year)
  - has_outcome(Work, OutcomeStatus)
  - has_genre(Work, Genre)

  - published_in(Work, Decade)

  - has_production_profile(Work, BPM, loudness)
  - spans_categories(Artist, [Category1, Category2, ...])

QUERIES (Examples):
  1. "What is the genre distribution of Pop nominees who won vs. who didn't?"
  2. "For artists spanning Rock and Country, what BPM patterns characterize each category?"
  3. "How has the average loudness of Grammy winners changed per decade per category?"
  4. "What (genre, category) associations are rare or absent?"
  5. "Which artists maintain the highest BPM consistency across their categories?"
```
